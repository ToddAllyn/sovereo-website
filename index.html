The Capital and Enterprise lens

For your money, whether or not you move

One lens, two questions about your money. Will it hold its value where you are headed, against inflation, a sliding currency, and capital controls? And can it work where it will actually grow, then come back to you when you want it? Those questions reach the pensioner stretching an income and the executive deploying capital alike, whether or not either ever moves. Sovereo scores all 195 countries on the part the relocation and offshore guides skip: whether you can get money in, put it to work, and get it back out.

Income that holds its value

Planning your move, in the middle of it, or already settled, a fixed or semi-fixed income has to hold up against inflation, a sliding currency, and capital controls that can freeze or trap your money. The lens shows where your money keeps its purchasing power and stays free to move when you need it.

Resources that keep working

You do not have to relocate to put hard-earned capital where it grows. The lens reads each country for how open it is to your money coming in and, just as important, whether you can take it back out when you choose.

Todd Allyn is an economist who taught the subject for a living, then made the cross-border move himself. He founded Sovereo Intelligence Research on a simple conviction: the biggest decisions in life deserve evidence, not opinion. Not theory, and not a dream sold from a distance, but practical tools, grounded in real research, that help people choose well.

He lives in Colombia, and that is the point, not a contradiction. Colombia sits mid-pack on the objective Index, yet weighted for his own life, family first, with portable income and the currency arbitrage the move makes possible, his diagnostic puts it at the top. That is the premise the company was built on: the right country is never the top of a worldwide ranking, it is the top of yours. He built the instrument that proves it, then moved where it pointed.

Twenty-four years in a classroom taught him to make hard things clear without dumbing them down.
