How to read this ledger

The scoring rule

A forecast is scored only on or after its stated date, never before. An open forecast is live, not a failure. When the date arrives, the call is marked correct or wrong against its explicit falsification condition, and the result stays on the record permanently.

The confidence scale

Every call carries one probability word on a fixed yardstick: remote near 5 percent, unlikely 25 to 35, realistic possibility 40 to 50, likely 55 to 75, highly likely 80 to 90, almost certain 95 or more. Confidence is revised on the evidence between issues, and every revision is recorded with a dated reason in the underlying ledger.

No cherry-picking

Every forecast the Brief and the Country Reports have issued appears here, open and closed. None is withdrawn, softened, or deleted after the fact. The misses will be published beside the hits with the same prominence, on their dates. That discipline, not any single call, is the product.

Read the calls before they score

The ledger is the receipt. The Sovereo Brief is where each call is made, argued, and dated, a week before the market or the headline catches up.
