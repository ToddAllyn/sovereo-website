How to read this ledger

The scoring rule

A forecast is scored only on or after its stated date. An open forecast is live, not a failure. When the date arrives, the call is marked correct or wrong against its explicit falsification condition, and the result stays on the record permanently.

The confidence scale

Every call carries one probability word on a fixed yardstick: remote near 5 percent, unlikely 25 to 35, realistic possibility 40 to 50, likely 55 to 75, highly likely 80 to 90, almost certain 95 or more. Confidence is revised on the evidence between issues, and the revision is shown (for example 004-1 moving from 72 to 52, or 008-1 from 60 to 72). Both the original and the revised number are kept.

The Brier score

The Brier score is the standard measure of forecast accuracy: the average squared gap between the confidence stated and what happened, where lower is better. A perfect record is 0, guessing 50 percent every time on a coin flip is 0.25. Sovereo publishes the number even when it is unflattering, because a record you can check is the entire point.

No cherry-picking

Every forecast the Brief has ever issued appears here. None is withdrawn, softened, or deleted after the fact. The misses will be published beside the hits with the same prominence. That discipline, not any single call, is the product.

Read the calls before they score

The ledger is the receipt. The Sovereo Brief is where each call is made, argued, and dated a week before the market or the headline catches up. One issue a week, decision-grade, with the reasoning shown.
